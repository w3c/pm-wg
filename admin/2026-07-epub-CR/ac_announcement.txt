[Draft] EPUB 3.4, EPUB 3.4 Reading Systems, EPUB Accessibility 1.2 are now Candidate Recommendation (Call for Implementations)


Dear Advisory Committee Representative,
[This announcement will be forwarded to W3C group chairs]

I am pleased to announce that the following documents are now a W3C
Candidate Recommendation Snapshot:

-  EPUB 3.4
   W3C Candidate Recommendation Snapshot 21 July 2026
   https://www.w3.org/TR/2026/CR-epub-34-20260721/

-  EPUB 3.4 Reading Systems
   W3C Candidate Recommendation Snapshot 21 July 2026
   https://www.w3.org/TR/2026/CR-epub-rs-34-20260721/

-  EPUB Accessibility 1.2
   W3C Candidate Recommendation Snapshot 21 July 2026
   https://www.w3.org/TR/2026/CR-epub-a11y-12-20260721/


The approval and publication are in response to this transition request:
   https://github.com/w3c/transitions/issues/816

Please provide feedback by 19 October 2026 as GitHub issues:
   https://github.com/w3c/epub-specs/issues/

Patent disclosures relevant to this specification may be found on the
Internationalization Working Group's patent disclosure page in
conformance with W3C policy:
   https://www.w3.org/groups/wg/pm/ipr

This Call for Implementations follows section 6.3.7 "Transitioning to
Candidate Recommendation" of the W3C Process Document:
  https://www.w3.org/policies/process/20250818/#transition-cr

Thank you,

For Philippe le Hégaret, VP, Technical Strategy,

EPUB 3.4
W3C Candidate Recommendation Snapshot, 21 July 2026

This Version:
   https://www.w3.org/TR/2026/CR-epub-34-20260721/
Latest Published Version:
   https://www.w3.org/TR/epub-34/

Abstract:
   EPUB® 3 defines a distribution and interchange format for digital
   publications and documents. The EPUB format provides a means of
   representing, packaging, and encoding structured and semantically
   enhanced web content — including HTML, CSS, SVG, and other
   resources — for distribution in a single-file container.

   This specification defines the authoring requirements for EPUB
   publications and represents the fourth major revision of the standard.

Status of This document:
  https://www.w3.org/TR/2026/CR-epub-34-20260721/#sotd

This Version:
   https://www.w3.org/TR/2026/CR-epub-rs-34-20260721/
Latest Published Version:
   https://www.w3.org/TR/epub-rs-34/

Abstract:
   EPUB® 3 defines a distribution and interchange format for digital
   publications and documents. The EPUB format provides a means of
   representing, packaging, and encoding structured and semantically
   enhanced web content — including HTML, CSS, SVG and other
   resources — for distribution in a single-file container.

    This specification defines the conformance requirements for
    EPUB 3 reading systems — the user agents that render EPUB publications.
